National Physics Talent Contest

Program Overview

The National Physics Talent Contest (NPTC) inspires Pakistani students for participation in the International Physics Olympiad. This regular annual event has been running since 1995 and is conducted in five comprehensive stages.

Contest Stages

  • Stage 1: 1000+ educational institutions nominate best students (75%+ marks in physics/math)
  • Stage 2: Screening test in major cities, top 50 students selected
  • Stage 3: Week-long intensive training camp at PIEAS (August)
  • Stage 4: Second camp in December for shortlisted students
  • Stage 5: Final camp to select 5-member Pakistani team

Training Features

  • Theory coaching in mornings, experiments in afternoons
  • Visits to experimental facilities in Nilore Complex
  • Evening meetings with renowned scientists
  • Daily themes named after famous scientists (Alberuni, Galileo, Newton)
  • Standard physics textbook prizes for all participants

Rewards & Recognition

  • Merit certificates for top performers
  • Cash prizes worth PKR 20,000 each for top 5 students
  • NPTC Alumni membership with quarterly newsletter
  • Additional week of coaching before International Olympiad

Home Institution: PIEAS

National Engineering Robotics Contest

Program Overview

National Engineering Robotics Contest is a joint project of National University of Sciences and Technology, STEM Careers Programme and Pakistan Science Foundation, to promote research in robotics and its related fields in Pakistan.

Competition Features

  • Integration and evaluation of mechanical and electronics designs
  • Control and path planning algorithms
  • Agent architectures development
  • Exciting new challenges every year
  • Nationwide participation from engineering students

Host Institution

Department of Mechatronics Engineering, CEME NUST welcomes participants to the annual NERC competition.

Growth & Impact

  • Increasingly popular among students
  • Growing participation from university engineering departments
  • Advancement of robotics community at wider scale
  • Common environment for design evaluation

Host: NUST - Department of Mechatronics Engineering

Design, Build & Fly Competition

Program Overview

DBFC is part of the National Engineering Competitions organized by GIK to groom youth by providing excellent opportunities for students and faculty from multiple disciplines to engage in creative activities.

Design Constraints

  • Aircraft must be in Pusher Configuration (Single/Twin Booms pusher)
  • Wingspan: Maximum 2.5 meters
  • Weight: Maximum 3 Kg for all missions
  • Battery: LiPo max 2200 mAh, max 12V
  • Transmitters with telemetry capability required

Mission Requirements

  • Ground rolling take-off and landing
  • Simulate take-off from small austere field
  • Must take off within 60 ft
  • Various mission scenarios

Objectives

  • Encourage youth participation in aerospace
  • Hunt talent for international competitions
  • Multidisciplinary collaboration
  • Creative problem solving

Organized by: GIK Institute

National Engineering Competition

Program Overview

National Engineering Competition (NEC) is launched on pilot scale under STEM Careers Programme, encouraging undergraduate engineering students to develop innovative solutions to problems of national interest.

Competition Structure

  • Annual themes regarding national problems
  • Interdisciplinary team participation
  • Supervision by faculty teachers
  • University-level and national-level competitions

Team Requirements

  • 3-4 students from different relevant departments
  • Open to all undergraduate engineering students
  • Fourth year students can use as senior project
  • Minimum 3 teams per participating university

Funding & Prizes

  • Project Funding: Up to PKR 10,000 per team
  • University Level: PKR 10,000, 7,000, 5,000
  • National Level: PKR 50,000, 30,000, 20,000
